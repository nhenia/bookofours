# 🕯️ The Grimoire of Time: Lunar Chronometry

In the *Book of Ours*, time is not a fixed grid, but a shifting tide. We follow the moon, not the sun. This document explains the underlying mechanics of our chronometry.

## 🌙 The 24.84-Hour Lunar Day

A solar day is 24 hours. A lunar day—the time it takes for the moon to return to the same position in the sky—is approximately **24 hours and 50 minutes** (24.8412 hours).

Because of this ~50-minute difference, our "Offices" drift against the solar clock. Matins might start at 2 AM today, but it will start at approximately 2:50 AM tomorrow.

## ⛪ The Eight Offices

The lunar day is divided into eight equal segments, mirroring the traditional monastic schedule:

1.  **Matins**: The deep night, the beginning of the lunar cycle. (0.00 - 0.125 progress)
2.  **Lauds**: The coming of the lunar light. (0.125 - 0.25 progress)
3.  **Prime**: The first office of the working day. (0.25 - 0.375 progress)
4.  **Terce**: Mid-morning reflection. (0.375 - 0.50 progress)
5.  **Sext**: The lunar zenith. (0.50 - 0.625 progress)
6.  **None**: Mid-afternoon work. (0.625 - 0.75 progress)
7.  **Vespers**: The winding down of the day. (0.75 - 0.875 progress)
8.  **Compline**: The final silence, completion. (0.875 - 1.00 progress)

## ⚖️ The Subtle Ruler

Each day is governed by a planetary "Subtle Ruler," determined by the solar day of the week:

- **Sunday**: Sun (Sol)
- **Monday**: Moon (Luna)
- **Tuesday**: Mars (Ares)
- **Wednesday**: Mercury (Hermes)
- **Thursday**: Jupiter (Zeus)
- **Friday**: Venus (Aphrodite)
- **Saturday**: Saturn (Cronus)
